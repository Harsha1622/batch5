Operator Precedence and Associativity

Operator Precedence

Operator precedence refers to the priority of operators in an expression. Operators with higher precedence are evaluated before operators with lower precedence. 


result = 2 + 3 * 4
print(result)

Multiplication has higher precedence than addition, so 3 * 4 is evaluated first.

Operator Associativity

Operator associativity defines the order of evaluation when multiple operators of the same precedence appear. 


value = 10 - 5 - 2
print(value)

Subtraction follows left-to-right associativity: (10 - 5) - 2

Operator Precedence Table (Highest to Lowest)

Precedence Operator Description Associativity
Highest () Parentheses Left to Right
** Exponentiation Right to Left
+, -, ~ Unary operators Right to Left
*, /, %, // Arithmetic operators Left to Right
